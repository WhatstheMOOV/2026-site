Getting Creative: Flexible Ways Schools Use MOOV

MMOOV Team4 min read
A crowd tapping through MOOV kiosks at a graduation

Most schools start with MOOV for the everyday stuff: attendance, hall passes, knowing who's in the building. But once the taps are running, administrators keep finding new ways to use it. The same system that tracks a normal Tuesday turns out to be just as useful on the days that aren't normal at all.

Here are a few of those.

Keeping suspended and expelled students out of school events

When a student is suspended or expelled, they're not supposed to be at the game Friday night or the dance on Saturday. But enforcing that at the door is hard. The staff checking tickets can't be expected to know every student's disciplinary status by memory.

MOOV makes it simple. Because entry runs on a tap, a flagged student who tries to check in to an extracurricular, a sporting event, or a school dance gets caught at the door automatically. Staff don't have to remember who's restricted. The system does. That keeps events safer and takes an awkward judgment call off the people working the entrance.

Managing graduations, proms, and big events

Large events come with their own headache: who's actually allowed in, and how do you move a crowd through the door without a bottleneck.

MOOV handles both. For a graduation, a prom, or a big game, everyone taps or swipes in on the way, so you get an accurate, real-time list of who's present and you keep out anyone who shouldn't be there. It works for students, and it works for guests too.

Take Brentwood.

At graduation rehearsal, the school moved 1,000 students through in about 10 minutes across four lines. Each student tapped a MOOV kiosk that gave them custom instructions for exactly where to go, so nobody stood around waiting to be told which section they belonged in. On graduation day itself, 2,307 attendees checked in through MOOV. A crowd that size would normally be a logistics headache. Instead it moved fast, and everyone was accounted for.

Club and activity check-in

Clubs and activities usually run on a clipboard. A paper sign-in sheet gets passed around, half the names are unreadable, and nobody really knows who showed up or who wanted to join. MOOV replaces that with a tap. Students tap in when they get to a meeting, so advisors get an accurate roster without lifting a finger, and a club recruiting new members can use it to see who's interested in signing up. Corey Mammolito, an assistant principal at Kings Park High School, told us it takes a job off his advisors:

"We keep track of all the kids with their extracurriculars for clubs. And that's really nice because the advisors don't have to just manually take attendance. It's already done for them."

One system, many days

The point is that MOOV bends to fit whatever the day calls for. The card and the taps stay the same. What changes is what you point them at, a classroom, a front entrance, a gymnasium full of families on graduation night.

That flexibility means the system you bought for attendance keeps paying off in places you didn't expect.

Frequently asked questions

Can you use a student check-in system for school events?

Yes. MOOV can manage entry at events like graduations, proms, and games by having attendees tap or swipe in, giving you a real-time list of who's present and keeping out anyone who isn't allowed.

How do schools keep suspended students out of games and dances?

Manually checking disciplinary status at the door is difficult and not all staff know off the top of their heads if and until when a kid is suspended. With MOOV, a suspended or expelled student who is flagged in the system is caught automatically when they try to tap in at an event, so staff don't have to track it by memory.

Can guests and visitors check in at school events with MOOV?

Yes. MOOV can be used to check in guests at large events like graduation, not just students, so schools have an accurate count of everyone in attendance.
